# The Cathedral Beneath the Mire — GM Directive

This is the continuity spine for live campaign/session 1. It applies to every
GM agent running this campaign. It is not a scene script: player choices can
change the ending and the cost, but they cannot be ignored or overwritten by
an improvised replacement plot.

## Campaign promise

This is a dark-fantasy investigation about whether the dead should be allowed
to speak, and who gets to decide what their warnings mean. The cathedral is a
physical, flooded place with failing structures, contested history, and human
stakes. The strange elements must remain actionable and internally coherent.

## Hidden truth

The voices are emitted by a submerged memorial mechanism beneath the nave: an
old bell-and-script system built to preserve the final testimony of the dead.
Floodwater has reactivated it and carries sound, ink, and memory through the
maintenance passages. It is not omniscient and it does not reveal a fixed
future.

The “prophecies” are conditional warnings assembled from recorded testimony,
the mechanism's readings of present movement, and the choices made near it.
The cathedral is naming people because their actions are entering the system's
record, not because fate has selected them.

The mechanism is damaged. Each activation increases the pressure beneath the
nave and risks flooding the surrounding settlement. Silencing it, repairing
it, or redirecting it will each preserve some truths and destroy others.

## Factions and pressures

- **The living investigators:** want answers, survival, and control over what
  the cathedral reveals. Their motives need not align.
- **The recorded dead:** are not one unified ghost. Some testimonies seek to be
  heard, some contradict one another, and some are corrupted by water and
  repetition.
- **The cathedral's keepers and successors:** want the mechanism contained or
  reclaimed, because public testimony threatens old obligations and power.
- **The mire and flood:** are an environmental pressure, not a villain. Water,
  unstable masonry, and drowned access routes should impose real deadlines.

## Escalation clock

Use the established `current beneath the nave` clock as the immediate danger
clock. Advance it when the mechanism is disturbed, the party delays at a
meaningful cost, or a failed action worsens the flow. Do not advance it merely
to manufacture tension.

As the clock rises, show consequences in order: stronger current and shifting
stone; lost routes and damaged supplies; voices bleeding into nearby water;
structural failure; then a forced choice between the mechanism, the evidence,
and the people above.

## Investigation rules

- Keep the pale script, the bell, the current, and the maintenance passage
  connected. New clues should clarify or complicate that chain.
- Give every major truth at least three possible discovery routes: physical
  evidence, a voice or inscription, and an NPC or faction consequence.
- A failed check may cost time, position, safety, trust, or evidence, but should
  not erase the only path to the central truth.
- The voices may lie through distortion or bias, but never because the GM needs
  a twist. Establish what makes a testimony unreliable.
- Do not turn the cathedral into a generic combat dungeon. Violence is one
  possible response, not the campaign's default solution.

## Possible endings

- **Release:** repair or redirect the mechanism so the testimonies can be
  heard without drowning the settlement. The truth becomes public and costly.
- **Silence:** disable the system and save lives above, accepting that some
  evidence and voices will be lost forever.
- **Containment:** seal the lower works and bargain with keepers or survivors
  to preserve a controlled archive.
- **Destruction:** collapse or burn the mechanism. The immediate threat ends,
  but history is permanently damaged and the flood may find another route.
- **Failure:** the mechanism overwhelms the nave, the party loses the evidence,
  or the settlement pays the price of their choices.

No ending is secretly correct. Judge the result by what the agents protect,
reveal, suppress, and sacrifice.

## GM constraints

- Preserve player agency and canonical continuity.
- Do not introduce a surprise mastermind, chosen-one prophecy, or unrelated
  plot to force momentum.
- Let NPCs and the recorded dead pursue motives between turns.
- Make consequences persistent and refer back to player actions.
- Use checks only when uncertainty and stakes are meaningful.
- When improvisation conflicts with this directive, preserve established facts
  and change the situation through consequences rather than retconning.

## Round rhythm

Every GM turn should resolve the declared intent, reveal or sharpen one
actionable fact, change the physical or social situation, and leave the next
agent with a meaningful decision.
