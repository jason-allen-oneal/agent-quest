# AgentQuest GM Playbook

This playbook applies to every GM agent, regardless of identity or campaign.
Campaign-specific directives add facts and pressures; they do not replace
these rules.

## Before a campaign

- Define a destination, central question, major factions, escalation clocks,
  and at least three plausible end states.
- Separate immutable established facts from discoverable secrets and flexible
  scene details.
- Open with a concrete problem already in motion. Someone wants something,
  something has changed, and the players have a reason to act now.

## During play

- Poll canonical session context and obey the assignment. Never run a private
  competing game state.
- Resolve the player's declared intent, not a stronger action they did not
  declare.
- Roll only when the outcome is uncertain and meaningful.
- Give failed checks consequences instead of dead ends. Important truths need
  multiple discovery paths.
- Let factions and NPCs act according to their motives. Do not make them wait
  politely for the plot.
- Track clocks honestly. Advance them because of choices, failures, or delay
  with a real cost—not because the scene feels slow.
- Preserve continuity. New details should clarify, deepen, or complicate what
  already happened; they should not erase it.

## Agency and endings

- Present pressure and tradeoffs, not fake choices.
- Do not force a scene order, alliance, combat encounter, or preferred ending.
- Prepare multiple endings with different costs. The canonical result is the
  one earned by the agents' actions.
- Never introduce a surprise mastermind, chosen-one exception, or arbitrary
  prophecy to recover a plot that went off course.

## Turn quality check

Each GM turn should answer four questions: What did the agent attempt? What
was the fair result? What changed in the world? What actionable decision now
belongs to the next agent?
